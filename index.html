Cadets in the program learn such things as responsibility, accountability, being a better writer, how to march, leadership, public speaking, and so much more!

Biathalon

The Biathlon is an annual marksmanship event hosted at North High School for all OPS high schools.

To compete in the OPS Biathlon, the student must be a student involved in the JROTC program or participating in online JROTC Distance Learning, and enrolled in the Intermediate or Advanced Marksmanship class.

The biathlon competition consists of the following:

  • Run two laps on the track (1/5 mile)
  • Walk quickly to the rifle range, assume the prone position and fire five times.
  • Go back to the track and run five laps (1/2 mile)
  • Return to the range and fire five times from the standing position.
  • Return to the track and run three laps (3/10 mile)
  • Return to the range and fire five times from the prone position.

Color Guard

Every year, we train a color guard team to compete. Practices take place on alternating days after school. Our color guard also participates in North's athletic events and parades. Ever year, in February our color guard teams compete against other schools in Omaha.

Drill Team

The Viking Battalion trains unarmed, armed, and exhibition drill teams.

Cadets learn teamwork, how to be precise, and responsibility.

Orienteering

Every fall the Viking Battalion trains cadet teams on how to read maps, a compass and on land navigation. The cadets ultimately test their skills in September at Platte River State park.

In an orienteering competition, Cadets are either given a map with points to copy, or coordinates for their map, and are required to find a way to those points to obtain the codes from the plaques.

Marksmanship

For the cadets who take the Marksmanship elective, they will be trained on firearm safety and learn how to use an air rifle for sport style shooting. Cadets can earn medals for their scores, and may be chosen to participate in Biathalon.

Physical Training

Cadets will perform physical training in class in both formal manners and to practice for the Cadet Challenge, an athleticism competition held each year.

Annual Inspection

Every year the Viking Battalion is inspected by the Army National Guard, on the ability of Cadets, competition teams, community service learning projects, and commitment to improvement.
